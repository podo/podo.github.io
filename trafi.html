Trafi — Finding the Better Ways of Moving Within a City

Product Design, Reseach, Branding, Design Systems

Introduction

There are multiple means of moving within a city — public transport (metro, trams, buses, trains), car sharing, car hailing, bike sharing and more. So, choosing the best way, depending on the day or time you are traveling might be difficult.

This gets even more complicated in a cities, where public transportation network is not developed — access to transport information is not available, schedules are changing based on the traffic conditions or just getting somewhere requires the combination of multiple transportations.

Patterns of moving variates not only per person — income or social level, genre, time of traveling, distance, understanding of comfort, but also per city — size, urban planning, number of transportation types, economical development level, seasons and more.

So, how to create an experience — global as a brand, local as a city and personal as unique individual, which would help you find the better ways of moving within a city?

Research & Analysis

Trafi started as a product mainly focused on public transportion in Baltic countries. This was very visible from both previous logo (a stickyman holding a bus handle), application structure and, even, user interface details.

As we were moving to bigger and more diverse markets, it was crushial to understand a differences of transportation within the cities, as well as, already existing pain points of current users.

For this we conducted a lots of user interviews, market and competitors research, talked with transportation agencies and local governments. We also traveled to some of the expansion cities — Jakarta, Bangalore, Delhi, Mumbai and Pune.

Definition of the Goals

After doing tens of user interviews, market and competitors research, BI analysis and several product ideations, we came with conclusion that in order to solve the users problem we need:

  • A flexible design system, which could adopt to differences in cities, but also would be easily scalable and maintainable.
  • An user experience, which would cover all major uses-cases and could be eventually personalized.
  • A brand, which would feel local, but would have the potential to become recognizable globally.

My goal was to focus on all three sections by designing interactive experiences for iOS, Android and Web, as well as preparing the brand for cross-market rollout.

By measuring the NPS rating change per cities, feedback on Google Play Store and iTunes App Store, organic growth, retention and adoption of integrated transport types, we would be able to understand how well we are succeeding in our mission.

Creation of the Fundamentals

In order to retain the existing user-base, it was natural to improve existing and already familiar flows, and only build on top of it.

Trafi is information-heavy product, so by mapping the information structure, as it heavily implies the structure and the herarchy of the screens, I was able to create a holistic view of the application.

As most of the information entities were already built around unique components (stop, schedule, track, vehicle), so the next step was to build a design system, which would allow me quickly iterate on recomposition and improvements of the screens and flows.

I’ve started with an audit of current screens and UI elements by fleshing out variations of atoms and molecules. Audit helped me to understand what kind of constrains the design system should have — sizes of texts and icons, colors palette, exact list of components and their properties.

Of course, by focusing on small components and not the screens or flows, you might loose the grasp of whole experience, so it was crushial to test newly created components as early as possible and, if needed, switch back to improve or totally rework them.

Also, the goal of the design system is not only to make experience more consistent by fabricating the constrains for your designs, but to improve the quality and speed of design to development processes. For this reason, I’ve created a single source of truth — a design system page for designers and developers, so everybody could share the vocabulary and constrains of the system. It also, allowed my team to start more close collaboration by involving everyone in design system creation and maintanance.

Once the basic components were ready, I was able to start working on improvements of the screens and flow itself, while in parallel, adding needed components into design system.

Exploration & Design

Nearly whole Trafi experience is divided into to groups of use-cases — the route search and the list of transportation schedules. The usage numbers were somewhat different per cities, but each of these two groups accounted for almost the half of time spend in the app, so older version — with five bottom tabs, forced our users to choose one of these two case, while totally hiding the second one. This also meant, that some types of transportation were not discovered.

So, I’ve started the improvements by exploration of a new home page, which offered way of accessing both groups of use-cases, displayed full selection of offered transportation types and had a beginnings for personalized experience — ways to add most frequently used searches (“To Home” and “To Work”).

Making the Global Brand

While I had some experience with branding projects from my previous work at Vinted, due to time constrains, we needed a help from experts on definition of the fundamental pillars for our company, mission statements, communication guidelines, as well as basic visual styleguide with logo, primary colors, imagery and art direction examples, so I could prepare the extensive set of material for brand and application launch across all our existing and new markets.

For that we’ve collaborated with amazing guys from Studio Kotostudiokoto.co, who had an opportunity to work on Airbnbstudiokoto.co/airbnb_trips and Fantastudiokoto.co/fanta rebranding projects. We’ve also asked Be&Dobeand.do team to help us with the brand strategy.

Having collected some data from user research and competitors analysis, I’ve prepared requirements for visual part of the brand:

  • As our user interface is icon-heavy and we want to feel as local as possible, we should focus on diverse photography.
  • Because we already use the colors of transportation brands and we want to avoid the use of traffic light colors, we should try finding a unique and easily recognizable palette for our brand.
  • Having in mind the information density in our product, UI typeface should be markably legible, slightly condensed and have great x-height.

Couple intensive months of collaborative work between the studios, our branding team and co-founders of Trafi, and we’ve received final version of styleguide, which I’ve used to produce final visuals for apps, web, company swag, outdoor and online marketing.

Conclusion

I left Trafi just before the launch of the new brand, however, updated app already showed very promising results — NPS and retention reached all time highs, organic growth started accelerating and hearing the feedback from the users, I was sure that we’ve fixed all the issues we planned to fix before the start of a project.

There is still a lot of space for improvements — more personalized home screen, optimized UI for older devices used in India, improvements in Map, better experience for web application, feed and disruptions rework.

I spent only 6 months working at Trafi, however, I’m very proud, what our team achieved in this — highly collaborative and agile enviroment. Designers and developers are collectively maintaining design systemui.trafi.com, brand looks fantastic and product has the best shape in years.

I’m use Trafi everyday and, more importantly, I see more and more people using it everyday, so it really helps to find the better ways of moving within a city!
